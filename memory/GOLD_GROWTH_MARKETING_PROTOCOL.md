## 1. FILOZOFIA GROWTH & MARKETING

### 1.1 Podstawowa Zasada
> „Growth to nie tylko więcej użytkowników. Growth to system, który konwertuje uwagę na wartość."

Protokół opiera się na **systematycznym podejściu** do zdobywania i utrzymywania użytkowników. Nie szukamy viralowych hacków — budujemy maszynę growthową.

### 1.2 Różnica: Marketing vs Growth

**Tradycyjny Marketing:**
- Focus: Brand awareness, reach
- Metryki: Impressions, clicks, likes
- Timeline: Quarterly campaigns
- Budżet: $$$ na ads

**Growth (Ten Protokół):**
- Focus: Acquisition, retention, revenue
- Metryki: CAC, LTV, churn, activation rate
- Timeline: Continuous experimentation
- Budżet: ROI-positive channels only

### 1.3 The Stealth Mode Strategy
> „We built & shipped in full stealth mode, no big launch, no threads... just iterating and letting users find it." — @code_rams

**Strategia TweetsMash/LinkedMash ($1.4k+ w 7 dni):**
- Build in stealth (bez hype'u)
- Iterate szybko
- Let users find it organically
- No big launch
- Focus na produkt, nie na marketing

**Wyniki:**
- $0 → $100k MRR w 8 miesięcy (przykład z branży)
- $0 → $4K MRR w 6 dni (przykład z branży)
- $39k revenue po 129 dniach vibe codingu

### 1.4 Build in Public vs Stealth

**Build in Public:**
- ✅ Szybsza walidacja idei
- ✅ Community feedback w czasie rzeczywistym
- ✅ Content marketing „za darmo"
- ❌ Competitors see your moves
- ❌ Pressure to show progress

**Stealth Mode:**
- ✅ Pełna kontrola nad narracją
- ✅ Competitors don't know what you're building
- ✅ Iterate bez presji publiczności
- ❌ Brak early feedback
- ❌ Trudniejszy start (cold start problem)

**Rekomendacja:** Start stealth → soft launch → build in public po PMF.

## 3. LANDING PAGES & CONVERSION

### 3.1 Landing Page Anatomy

**Struktura High-Converting Landing Page:**

1. **Hero Section**
   - Headline: Clear value proposition (H1)
   - Subheadline: Problem + Solution
   - CTA Button: Primary action
   - Social Proof: Trust badges

2. **Problem Agitation**
   - „You know this pain?"
   - 3-4 pain points your audience faces

3. **Solution Presentation**
   - How you solve the problem
   - 3-6 key features/benefits

4. **Social Proof**
   - Testimonials with faces
   - Logos of customers
   - Stats („Join 10,000+ users")

5. **How It Works**
   - 3-4 step process
   - Visual + text

6. **Pricing/CTA**
   - Clear tiers
   - Risk reversal (money-back guarantee)

7. **FAQ**
   - Address objections
   - Remove friction

8. **Final CTA**
   - Repeated CTA
   - Urgency/scarcity (optional)

### 3.2 Conversion Principles

**The Calm Principle (@basit_designs):**
> „When the landing page feels this calm, investors assume the business is stable."

**Key Elements:**
- Whitespace jako aktywny element
- Ograniczona paleta (2-3 primary colors)
- Jasna hierarchia typograficzna
- Brak wizualnego szumu

**Scott Belsky's Rule:**
> „People are lazy, selfish and vain. Your onboarding should play into all of this."

- **Lazy:** One click to goal
- **Selfish:** „What's in it for me" first
- **Vain:** Prestige elements, social proof

### 3.3 CTA Best Practices

❌ „Submit"  
✅ „Start Free Trial"

❌ „Learn More"  
✅ „See How It Works"

❌ „Buy Now"  
✅ „Get Started — 14 Days Free"

**Button Formula:**
- Action verb + Benefit + Risk reversal
- Example: „Get Your First 100 Users — Free"

### 3.4 Friction Killers

**Remove These Barriers:**
- [ ] Minimum form fields (email only for start)
- [ ] No credit card required for trial
- [ ] Clear next steps after signup
- [ ] No hidden fees
- [ ] Instant value delivery
- [ ] Mobile-optimized flow

## 4. PRICING STRATEGY

### 4.1 The Ultimate Mobile App Pricing Cheatsheet (@Hartdrawss)

| Model | Cena | Use Case | Conversion |
|-------|------|----------|------------|
| **One-time purchase** | $X | Dead for SaaS (except niche tools) | Low |
| **Freemium** | $0 | Only if free tier is INSANELY generous | <5% |
| **$9/month** | $9 | Feels cheap but kills perceived value | Medium |
| **$29/month** | $29 | **Sweet spot for indie tools** | **High** |
| **$49-79/month** | $49-79 | Serious B2B features | Medium-High |
| **$99+/month** | $99+ | Only if you solve $10k+ problems | Low |

### 4.2 Pricing Rules

1. **Always have annual discount (20-30%)**
   - People love „save $X"
   - Improves cash flow

2. **14-day trial beats lifetime access**
   - Forces real usage
   - Reduces tire kickers

3. **Three tiers maximum**
   - Good / Better / Best
   - Middle tier = most popular (anchor)

4. **Perceived value > Actual cost**
   - Price based on value delivered, not your costs
   - $29/month is cheap if you save 5 hours

### 4.3 Pricing Psychology

**The Decoy Effect:**
- Tier 1: $19/month (Basic)
- Tier 2: $49/month (Pro) ← Most popular
- Tier 3: $99/month (Enterprise)

**The Rule of 9:**
- $29 > $30 psychologically
- $99 > $100

**Free vs Paid Perception:**
- Free = Low quality (in minds of some)
- Paid = Serious tool
- Freemium = Try before you buy

## 5. RETENTION & ENGAGEMENT

### 5.1 The Retention Hook Framework

**7 Types of Retention Hooks:**

| Hook Type | Mechanism | Example |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| **Progress** | Users see their advancement | Fitness apps, courses |
| **Streak** | Don't break the chain | Duolingo, GitHub |
| **Social** | Friends are using it | Instagram, Strava |
| **Investment** | Users put in effort/data | Notion, Spotify playlists |
| **Utility** | Daily use tool | Email, Calendar |
| **Variable Reward** | Unpredictable positive outcomes | Slot machines, social feeds |
| **Loss Aversion** | Fear of losing progress | Subscription services |

### 5.2 Feature Categories for Retention

**Journey Completers:**
- Close loops where users start but can't finish
- Example: Save draft, reminders, follow-ups

**Retention Hooks:**
- Give users a reason to come back without being reminded
- Example: Daily digest, weekly summary

**Delight Moments:**
- Small, unexpected touches
- Example: Celebrate milestones, personal notes

**Friction Killers:**
- Remove steps, reduce decisions
- Example: One-click actions, smart defaults

**Monetization Enablers:**
- Features so valuable users WANT to pay
- Example: Advanced analytics, priority support

### 5.3 Activation Metrics

**The Aha! Moment:**
- Moment when user understands value
- Must happen in first session
- Examples:
  - Slack: First message sent
  - Twitter: First follower
  - Dropbox: First file shared

**Activation Checklist:**
- [ ] Define your „aha!" moment
- [ ] Measure time to activation
- [ ] Optimize onboarding to reach it faster
- [ ] Remove steps between signup and activation
- [ ] Celebrate activation moment with user

### 5.4 Churn Prevention

**Early Warning Signals:**
- Decreased login frequency
- Feature usage drop
- Support ticket increase
- Payment failures

**Intervention Strategies:**
- Personal outreach (high-value customers)
- Win-back email campaigns
- Special offers
- Exit surveys (learn why they leave)

- „[your brand] alternative"

## 7. CONTENT & COMMUNITY

### 7.1 Content Marketing Framework

**The Content Pillar Strategy:**

1. **Pillar Content (1x/month)**
   - Long-form (2000+ words)
   - Comprehensive guides
   - SEO-optimized
   - Example: „Complete Guide to Fitness App Marketing"

2. **Cluster Content (4x/month)**
   - Blog posts related to pillar
   - 800-1200 words
   - Link to pillar
   - Example: „10 Landing Page Tips for Fitness Apps"

3. **Social Content (Daily)**
   - Threads on X/Twitter
   - Short tips
   - Behind the scenes
   - Quick wins

### 7.2 Community Building

**Build in Public Checklist:**
- [ ] Share wins (and losses)
- [ ] Show process, not just results
- [ ] Engage with comments daily
- [ ] Help others without expecting return
- [ ] Be consistent (daily posts)
- [ ] Share numbers (revenue, users)

**Community Platforms:**
1. **X/Twitter** — Best for B2B, indie hackers
2. **Discord** — Deep engagement, support
3. **Reddit** — Niche communities, authentic

### 7.3 Content Distribution

**Distribution Channels:**

## 8. PRODUCT STRATEGY

### 8.1 Feature Intelligence Framework (@kloss_xyz)

**The Product Strategist Prompt:**

> „You combine the user obsession of Steve Jobs, the systems thinking of Tobi Lütke, the growth instincts of Brian Chesky, and the simplicity discipline of Dieter Rams."

**Three Gates for Every Feature:**
1. Does it serve the user journey?
2. Does it compound the value of what already exists?
3. Can it ship without breaking what works?

**Think in:**
- User journeys, not feature lists
- Compounding value, not isolated additions
- Phases, not dumps

### 8.2 Feature Planning Template

**FEATURE_PLAN_[YYYYMMDD].md:**

1. **Executive Summary** — 3-5 sentences. Biggest opportunity right now.
2. **Current State** — What's working, what's missing, what's at risk.
3. **Phase 1: Ship This Week** — High impact, low effort. 3-5 features max.
4. **Phase 2: Ship This Sprint** — More effort, significant value. 4-6 features.
5. **Phase 3: Ship This Quarter** — Strategic investment. 3-5 features max.
6. **Parking Lot** — Ideas for later.
7. **Rejected Ideas** — Cut with reasoning.
8. **Dependency Map** — What must be built before what.

### 8.3 User Research Questions

**Ask These Before Building:**
- Where do users get stuck, confused, or dead-ended?
- What features are 80% done but missing the last 20%?
- What would make a user show this app to a friend?
- What would make a user come back tomorrow without being reminded?
- What would make a user pay — or pay more — without hesitation?
- What does NO competitor offer that the user journey demands?

## PODSUMOWANIE

**Gold Growth & Marketing Protocol** to kompletny system zdobywania i utrzymywania użytkowników:

1. **Foundation** (Product-Market Fit) — bez tego nic nie działa
2. **Activation** (onboarding, first value) — konwertuj signup na usera
3. **Acquisition** (channels, content) — zdobywaj użytkowników
4. **Retention** (hooks, engagement) — utrzymuj ich
5. **Revenue** (pricing, upsell) — monetyzuj
6. **Referral** (word of mouth) — niech rosną organicznie

### Golden Rules:

1. **Stealth First** — Build, iterate, let users find you
2. **One Channel** — Master one before adding others
3. **PMF > Everything** — Retention beats acquisition
4. **$29 Sweet Spot** — Optimal pricing for indie tools
5. **Build in Public** — But only after initial traction
