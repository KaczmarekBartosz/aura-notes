## Training: Minimal Fatigue, Maximum Growth

### Core Concept
Low volume, high intensity training achieves similar strength outcomes with significantly less time and fatigue.

### Key Research
**Morton et al. (2016):**
- 1 set per exercise (13 min/session) = similar strength gains as 5 sets (68 min)
- Hypertrophy follows dose-response (more volume = more growth)
- Strength has a threshold effect (minimum dose sufficient)

**Schoenfeld et al. (2015):**
- High intensity (90% 1RM, low volume) > High volume (70% 1RM) for strength
- Less cortisol stress with high intensity approach
- Lean arm mass: +5.2% (high intensity) vs +2.2% (high volume)

### Integration with Gold Protocol
```
Deficit Phase (Fat Loss):
- Training: 1-2 sets to failure, 85-90% 1RM, 25-35 min
- Rationale: Less fatigue = better compliance during caloric restriction

Maintenance Phase:
- Training: 3 sets, 8-12 reps, 70-80% 1RM, 40-50 min
- Rationale: Sustainable long-term approach

Surplus Phase (Growth):
- Training: 3-4 sets, 8-12 reps, 70-75% 1RM, 55-60 min
- Rationale: Higher volume for hypertrophy
```

### Key Principles
1. **Minimum Effective Dose (MED):** Muscle needs stimulation, not destruction
2. **Maintenance vs Growth:** Maintain muscle at 1/3 to 1/9 of original building volume
3. **Fatigue Management:** High volume = high cortisol, long recovery
4. **Time Efficiency:** 3×13 min/week can match 3+ hours of traditional training

### Aurafit Implementation Ideas
- Auto-regulate volume based on caloric deficit level
- "MED Calculator" - show minimum volume to maintain results
- Periodization: High volume weeks → Deload (50%) → Moderate → Maintenance break
- Track subjective fatigue (RPE) to adjust volume automatically
